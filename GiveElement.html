CC103 -- Computer Programing 2

Computer Programming II explores and builds skills in C++ and Java. The study of C++ provides a basic understanding of the fundamentals of procedural program development using structured, modular concepts. Emphasizes logical program design involving user-defined functions and standard structure elements.

GE_4 -- Purposive Communication

Purposive Communication is a three-unit course that develops students' communicative competence and enhances their cultural and intercultural awareness through multimodal tasks that provide them opportunities for communicating effectively and appropriately to a multicultural audience in a local or global context.

GE Elec 1 -- Living in the IT Era

This course explains how the influence of Information Technology defines the way we live today. Identify the different discoveries and inventions on computers that help mold Information Technology as we know it. The students of this course will be acquainted with the history of computers and information technology.

WEB_1 -- Fundamentals of Website Develpment

This course teaches the fundamentals of front-end programming. Students will learn to build websites using HTML and CSS technologies. The course will help the students to learn how to style websites using CSS including learning to build responsive design websites.

IS 101 -- Fundamentals of Informations Systems

Concepts and applications of Management Information Systems. Introduction to hardware, software, data, and file concepts. Microcomputer applications software including word processing, spreadsheet, data base, Internet, and presentation software.

PATHFIT 2 -- Exercise-based Fitness Activity

Learners will undertake multiple and a variety of exercise programs that include mobility and flexibility training, running, speed-agility-quickness training, and when ready, power training. These programs are aimed at maintaining and/or enhancing musculoskeletal and cardiorespiratory fitness.

NSTP 2 -- Literacy Training Service 2

NSTP 2 classes are mainly concerned with community engagement with the help of partner communities and organizations. CWTS and LTS students are required to conduct civic welfare and literacy activities or projects as part of the required 40-64 hours of community engagement.
